Part one of Satyajit Ray's Apu Trilogy.

Ray’s debut film is the story of a small boy, Apu, and his life with his impoverished Brahmin family in a rural Bengal village. Ravi Shankar (then unknown) provides the score. (1955, 122 min, 35mm)

Restored by the Satyajit Ray Preservation Project through a collaboration of the Academy Film Archive, the Merchant-Ivory Foundation and the Film Foundation. Prints courtesy of the Academy Film Archive.
